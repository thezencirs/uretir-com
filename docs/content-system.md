# Content System

## Purpose

The content system turns Uretir from a stream of posts into a navigable map of production knowledge. Every article should answer a clear question and create a path to the next useful question.

The target is not a blog. It is a manufacturing knowledge platform capable of serving Turkey's production questions at large scale without sacrificing quality or editorial accountability.

## The five lenses

| Lens | Primary question | Typical entities |
| --- | --- | --- |
| NE URETIR | What is being made? | products, tools, materials, technologies, methods |
| KIM URETIR | Who makes it? | people, teams, companies, universities, factories |
| NASIL URETIR | How is it made? | processes, workflows, skills, systems, case studies |
| NEREDE URETIR | Where does it happen? | cities, regions, facilities, ecosystems, supply chains |
| NEDEN URETIR | Why does it matter? | problems, incentives, impacts, opportunities, strategy |

These lenses are not separate silos. A profile of a factory can connect to the products it makes, the methods it uses, the region it operates in, and the problem it solves. The relationship is more valuable than the isolated page.

Every company, product, technology, industry, factory, and article must be mapped to one or more of these lenses. The full entity and relationship contract lives in [knowledge-graph.md](./knowledge-graph.md).

## Content pillars

- Production and manufacturing
- Technology and AI
- Engineering and infrastructure
- Design and architecture
- Business, investment, and entrepreneurship
- Energy, sustainability, and climate capability
- Opportunities: incentives, grants, exports, and financing

## Content types

- Explainers: durable foundations for a topic.
- Analysis: an informed interpretation of a change, market, or technology.
- Field guide: practical process, checklist, or comparison.
- Company or expert profile: verified context about a producer.
- Case study: what happened, why, results, and transferable lessons.
- Tool page: use case, audience, limitations, alternatives, and updates.
- Opportunity page: eligibility, deadline, source, and action path.
- Collection: an editorially curated route through multiple pieces of knowledge.
- Entity page: an evidence-backed, canonical page for a company, product, industry, factory, city, technology, machine, material, or AI tool.

## Editorial standards

Every content item needs an intended audience, source standard, owner, update expectation, and relationship to a cluster. Time-sensitive claims must include dates and source context. Distinguish reporting, analysis, sponsored material, and AI-assisted drafts clearly.

Each brief must also identify its primary organic-growth contribution: discoverability, deeper reading, return behavior, internal-link coverage, authority, community contribution, or AI retrieval value. Content that does not strengthen at least one of these assets should not enter the publishing queue.

An article must name the entities it explains or connects before drafting. At minimum, the brief records relevant companies, products, categories, industries, factories, cities, technologies, machines, raw materials, and AI tools; it explicitly notes when a class is not relevant. This prevents accidental orphan content and makes internal linking intentional.

The source, review, media, FAQ, freshness, and publication contract is defined in [editorial-engine.md](./editorial-engine.md). The editorial engine is mandatory for new reference content.

## Lifecycle

```text
Discover -> Brief -> Research -> Draft -> Edit -> Publish -> Observe -> Refresh / Consolidate / Retire
```

Content is not complete at publication. Refresh high-value pages as conditions change, consolidate overlapping pages, and redirect retired URLs when a better destination exists.

## AI-assisted content

AI may assist with research organization, outlines, translation, classification, and drafting. It may not replace accountable editorial review, fabricate citations, or publish unverified claims. The human owner remains responsible for accuracy, utility, and tone.
