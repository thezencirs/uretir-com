# Sustainable Organic Growth Strategy

## Operating objective

Uretir's primary operating objective is sustainable organic growth and search authority in manufacturing, industry, technology, and production. The company builds durable discovery and authority before it scales community and monetization.

Priority order:

1. Traffic first.
2. Authority second.
3. Community third.
4. Monetization follows naturally from useful, trusted demand.

This order does not permit low-quality traffic tactics. Growth must increase the usefulness, trustworthiness, and connectedness of the knowledge system.

The strategic destination is to become the default trusted destination in Turkey for production-related questions. The product is a manufacturing knowledge platform, not a blog with isolated posts.

## Mandatory feature gate

Every proposed feature, content format, technical investment, and experiment must materially contribute to at least one outcome below. If it cannot, postpone it.

| Outcome | What it means | Example evidence |
| --- | --- | --- |
| More Google traffic | More qualified organic discovery for durable user questions | indexed landing pages, ranking gains, qualified organic sessions |
| Longer user sessions | Readers can continue learning without friction or dead ends | engaged time, scroll completion, meaningful next-page journeys |
| More returning visitors | Uretir creates enough recurring value to earn another visit | repeat visitor rate, saved-content return, newsletter or notification return |
| Better internal linking | Knowledge pages reveal relevant entities and next questions | internal click-through, hub coverage, orphan-page reduction |
| Stronger authority | Sources, expertise, editorial quality, and entity coverage become more credible | citations, branded search, backlinks, verified expert/company coverage |
| Higher community engagement | Members make or sustain useful connections and contributions | quality comments, follows, collection shares, verified contributions |
| Better AI usefulness | AI answers become more grounded, personalized with consent, and actionable | sourced-answer quality, task completion, correction rate, retention |

## Feature brief requirement

Before implementation, every non-trivial feature brief must name:

- The primary growth outcome and any secondary outcomes.
- The user behavior expected to change.
- The metric, baseline, target direction, and measurement window.
- The content, technical, or distribution dependency required for success.
- Why this is more valuable now than the next best alternative.

Features without a credible answer are not rejected forever; they are postponed until their purpose becomes clear.

## Guardrails

- Never trade user trust, accessibility, privacy, or factual quality for a short-term metric.
- Prefer compounding assets: durable articles, entity pages, topic clusters, source relationships, saved knowledge, and reusable AI retrieval.
- Treat entity coverage and relationship quality as acquisition assets. See [knowledge-graph.md](./knowledge-graph.md) for the canonical model.
- Do not count impressions, raw pageviews, or empty interactions as success without a qualified behavior signal.
- Community mechanics must encourage useful contribution, not engagement farming.
- Monetization may not distort editorial ranking, recommendations, or source transparency.

## Review cadence

Review this strategy monthly alongside the roadmap. Review feature outcomes after the agreed measurement window and explicitly decide whether to expand, iterate, maintain, or retire the work.
